Delhi - 3 Nights 07 Oct - 10 Oct

Delhi: Mysterious eternity – Imperial past and pulsating present! India’s capital Delhi, a product of over 5000 years history, houses two World Heritage sites and the finest museums. Shops offer traditional and contemporary handicrafts. Its plethora of fine dining restaurants and a throbbing nightlife add zest to this historical city.

Included Activities

Highlights of Delhi

Whether you want a whirlwind tour or wish to enjoy an in-depth experience of Delhi’s numerous sights, the choice is yours! Discover the Old Town with its bustling Chandni Chowk, the imposing Red Fort and Jama Masjid. Feast at the colorful, open-air food and craft bazaar of Dili Haat. Wander Lodhi Gardens or discover many architecturally-beautiful temples - from the Baha'i Lotus Temple to ISKCON temple or Laxminarayan Temple. Visit National Museum, Bangala Sahib, the Gandhi Memorial at Raj Ghat or the Parliament Museum in Parliament Street. Kindly note that Red Fort is closed on Mondays.

Agra - 1 Night 10 Oct - 11 Oct

Drive from Delhi to Agra (205 km, 4 hrs)

Agra: Unravel the sheer charm of white marble! Layers of history are peeled away to reveal an amazing tapestry of life, for hiding behind the heat and chaos is a stunning monument etched in cool white marble – the wonder that is called the Taj Mahal – a World Heritage site, an architectural marvel and a testimony of undying love.

Included Activities

Highlights of Agra

Romance at sunrise and sunset and have your audience with the world’s greatest monument of love, the white marble Mughal mausoleum of the Taj Mahal, grandly overlooking the Yamuna River. This is not the only UNESCO World Heritage site to explore in Agra - you can witness the divine red sandstone Agra Fort, also a player in this renowned undying love story. Head to the Akbar Mausoleum in Sikandra, located in the suburbs. Not far away, the historic deserted city of Fatehpur Sikri is a must-see! If you wish, discover this fabled city with a ride in a decorated tonga (horse drawn carriage)!

Highlights of Fatehpur Sikri

Discover the former Mughal capital of Fatehpur Sikri, which is literally translated as City of Victory. Built and founded in the 16th century by the third Mughal Emperor, Akbar, his complex of monuments and temples was a planned imperial city. Akbar, who held ideals of a syncretistic faith, hoped to bring leaders of many religions together here. Unfortunately just ten years after completion the city was deserted due to drought and water shortages. Now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it’s a marvellous stop between Agra to Jaipur. Marvel at Buland Darwaza, the main entrance to this ancient city, and visit the elegant and sacred Jama Masjid (Friday Mosque). Spend time exploring the Tomb of Shaikh Salim Chisti, and Birbal Bhawan, an intricate building dedicated to Akbar's greatest court advisor. Venture into the inner court and harems where Akbar’s queen Jodha Bai, and other wives resided.

HIGHLIGHTS

  • Taj Mahal
  • Agra Fort
  • Fatehpur Sikri
  • Akbar Mausoleum or Sikandra Fort
  • Tonga Ride

Jaipur - 3 Nights 11 Oct - 14 Oct

Drive from Agra to Jaipur via Fatehpur Sikri (250 km, 5 hrs)

Jaipur: The ‘eternal Pink city’! Enclosed within hills, this’ rose pink’ city founded by Maharaja Jai Singh II, is Rajasthan’s capital. The whole city was painted pink by Man Singh when the Prince of Wales and later Edward VII, visited Jaipur in 1876. Today, every home is obliged by law to maintain this façade.

Included Activities

Highlights of Jaipur

Venture into regality! Explore Amber Fort, a fabulous sandstone fort complex that overlooks the city. Visit Albert Hall, the state museum of Rajasthan with its vast collection of interesting regional Indian artifacts. Observe the aesthetically beautiful honeycombed façade of Palace of Winds (Hawa Mahal), and the architecturally rich City Palace with its many regal courtyards, temples and pavilions, situated in the heart of the old city. Head to the world’s largest stone observatory – the Jantar Mantar. Discover Govind Devji Temple, dedicated to Lord Krishna, the Lakshmi Narayan and Ganesh Temples. Visit the amazing Jaigarh and Nahargarh Forts. Venture to the classical 1960s movie theater, Raj Mandir Cinema to absorb the fanfare of Bollywood royalty!

HIGHLIGHTS

  • Amber Fort
  • City Palace
  • The Palace of Winds
  • Jantar Mantar

Important Information

Important Notes...

Baggage allowance in India for international flights varies depending upon which airline and class you are traveling with. Kindly note that your domestic flight baggage allowance is usually less. As a rule of thumb, most airlines in India have a maximum baggage allowance of 15 kg (33 pounds) and you may be subject to excess weight baggage charges if you exceed this.

Don’t Forget To...

Apply for your visa

Travelers from most countries will need a tourist visa to India, which can be obtained from the Indian embassy in your country of residence or. U.S., Canadian and Australian citizens may also register for a "visa on arrival" online under https://indianvisaonline.gov.in/ (for stays up to 30 days). Please take care of this well in advance, as embassies in some countries may not be able to rush visa applications. Important: Please note that a permit is required for guests visiting Sikkim and we suggest that you apply for this at the same time as your visa. This permit incurs an additional fee. Further information about how and where to apply can be found at: http://sikkim.nic.in/homedept/ilpfaqs.htm. All hotels in Darjeeling & Sikkim require a passport size photograph of you at the time of check in.

Purchase travel insurance
